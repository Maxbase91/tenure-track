# Tenure Track — Reworked Systems & Events (v2)

---

## 1. Resources

| Resource | Unit | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Time** | weeks (small actions in days/hours) | Per-term budget you allocate. ~15 weeks/term. |
| **Personal money** | £ | Your stipend/salary. Pays your life. Low → Morale pain. |
| **Research funding** | £ | Grant money. Pays reagents, kit, conference trips. |
| **Reputation** | h-index-ish | Unlocks grants, students, journals, invited talks. Win condition. |
| **Knowledge** | results | Work-in-progress → papers. Scoopable if hoarded. |
| **Morale** | 0–100 | Wellbeing. Hits 0 → **Burnout**. |
| **Workload** | 0–100 | Accumulating stress. Above 70 → Morale bleeds. At 100 → forced Burnout. |
| **Coffee** | cups | Spend a cup → extra Time this term, but +Workload. The caffeine loan. |
| **Students** | people | Generate Knowledge; have their own loyalty + morale. |

**Realistic money scale (UK):** PhD stipend ~£19k/yr · postdoc ~£40k · lecturer ~£50k · −80 freezer ~£9k · confocal £250k · reagent campaign £3k–10k · pump-priming grant £25k · project grant (BBSRC/MRC) £600k · ERC/Wellcome £2M · conference reg £550 + travel £1,200 · open-access APC £3k (Nature-tier £9,500) · predatory journal £600.

**Realistic time costs:** experiment campaign 3 weeks · write a paper 3 weeks · write a grant 4–6 weeks · conference 1 week travel (+ prep by tier) · mentoring chat half a day · reading the literature a few hours · teaching block 2 weeks.

---

## 2. The Workload–Coffee–Morale loop (the burnout engine)

- Every action adds Workload. Teaching and admin add a lot. Overtime (via Coffee) adds more.
- Each term, if Workload > 70, you lose Morale.
- **Coffee**: 1 cup buys back ~2–3 days of Time this term, +Workload. Cheap to buy. Over-rely → a "Caffeine Crash" event (Workload spike, Morale drop). Realistic.
- Morale recovers from holidays, good events, a kind lab, finishing a term under-loaded.
- **Burnout** fires when Morale = 0 OR Workload = 100. The system biting back.

This is the spine: you *can* push hard for output, but the bill always comes due. Strategy = managing the bill, not avoiding it.

## 3. Conferences (tier × who-pays)

**Participation tier** — gated by Reputation, escalating prep + payoff:

| Tier | Prep | Reward | Gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Poster | 2 days | small Rep, networking | none |
| Short talk (12 min) | 1 week | more Rep, a lead | Rep ≥ 4 |
| Full talk (40 min) | 2 weeks | big Rep | Rep ≥ 8 |
| Invited speaker | 2 weeks | big Rep + prestige + a recruiting boost | Rep ≥ 12 (you get *invited*) |

**Who pays for the trip:**
- **Travel grant** — apply (compete), if won the trip is free.
- **Lab funds it** — comes out of research funding (~£1,750 all-in).
- **Out of pocket** — hits personal money + −Morale (you resent it), but you go anyway.

So each conference is a small matrix: how big a slot can you land, and how do you afford it.

## 4. Role-aware events + the karma layer

Events read your career stage. The hierarchy looks different from the bottom and the top.

- **As PhD / Postdoc:** you're acted upon — credit taken, teaching dumped on you, made to wait.
- **As PI:** *you* make those calls. Take credit or share it. Protect a student from teaching or offload it onto them. Fund their conference or make them self-fund.

**Karma is mechanical, not moral flavour.** Each PI choice shifts your students' **loyalty**. Loyal students: cover for you in Burnout, save samples at 3am, stay when poached. Exploited students: leave, underperform, let you sink. Evil play is faster output now; kind play is a lab that survives crises. Both are viable; they fail differently.

## 5. Quick start — four entry points

| Scenario | You start as | The squeeze | Win |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The PhD Crunch** | 3rd-year PhD, stipend running out | publish + write up before funding ends | submit a thesis-worthy paper |
| **The Postdoc Gamble** | postdoc, one year on contract | land a first-author paper good enough for a faculty job | get the job offer |
| **New PI, Empty Lab** | freshly hired lecturer, no team, no grant | win first grant + recruit before probation review | pass probation |
| **The Tenure Sprint** | established, pre-tenure | out-run the committee | get tenure |

Career mode = the full arc from PhD to tenure decision with everything chained.

## 6. The cast (UK, invented)

- **Prof. Alan Pemberton** — your supervisor when junior. Takes credit, means well, mostly absent.
- **Dr. Sarah Whitfield** — decent, overstretched, never reads your drafts in time.
- **Prof. Margaret Critchley** — sits on the tenure committee. Whispers.
- **Dr. James Holloway** — the rival lab across the corridor. Always one preprint ahead.
- **Your students (when you're a PI):** Chloe Adeyemi, Ben Hartley, Priya Nair, Tom Fletcher.
- **Aisha Khan** — the undergrad who's secretly better than all of them.
- **Dr. Stephen Marsh** — the collaborator who never, ever replies.
- **Sir Geoffrey Ashworth** — a donor with opinions.
- **Ellie** — your partner. *(Name is set or randomised at game start. UK cast by default.)*

## 7. Reworked events

Same rules: always a choice, choices depend on your state, some light a fuse.

### 1. Reviewer 2
*Back from review. Reviewer 2 has concerns. All of them. Reviewer 1 said accept. Reviewer 3 reviewed a different paper.*
- **Rebuttal (2 weeks, +Workload).** If Rep ≥ 5, accepted; else rejected anyway.
- **Run the three demanded experiments (6 weeks + £8k reagents).** Accepted. *Fuse: the new data reveals something bigger in 2 terms.*
- **Dump it in a lesser journal (3 days).** +small Rep only.

### 2. The −80 Freezer Dies
*02:47. The freezer hit −11°C. Your samples are sweating.*
- **Emergency callout (£4,000) + salvage.** Keep your Knowledge. (Locked if research funding is short.)
- **Let it go.** Lose half your unpublished Knowledge.
- *If a student has high loyalty:* **Ben drove in at 3am and saved most of it.** Lose nothing; Ben's loyalty rises. (Only offered if you've mentored lately.)

### 3. "Esteemed Professor"
*The International Journal of Frontier Science Advances admires your seminal work. Publication fee: £600.*
- **Delete.** Correct.
- **Pay and pad the CV (£600).** A "paper" worth nothing. *Fuse: spotted at your next review → −3 Rep.*

### 4. Whose Name Goes First? *(role-aware)*
**If PhD/Postdoc —** *Pemberton has put his name first on your result.*
- **Swallow it.** −Morale, but he backs your next grant → +funding later.
- **Confront him.** +Morale, lose his mentorship bonus.
- **Tell the collaborator whose idea it was.** *Fuse: authorship dispute later.*

**If PI —** *It's Chloe's breakthrough. The paper needs a first author.*
- **Chloe first (friendly).** Her loyalty + Morale rise; your Rep gain is smaller. Karma+.
- **You first (evil).** Big Rep now; Chloe's loyalty drops. *Fuse: she won't cover your next Burnout, and may leave.*
- **"Co-first authors."** The diplomatic dodge. Small Rep cost, everyone stays okay.

### 5. Ellie's Offer
*Your partner Ellie just got the offer of their career. In Edinburgh. You're in London.*
- **Relocate with them.** Lose a career stage of lab progress, restart with +Morale. The brutal honest one.
- **Long-distance.** Keep the lab; −Morale every term for 4 terms; +Workload (the commuting).
- **They turn it down for you.** Keep everything. *Fuse: a resentment event in 3 terms unless you've spent Time on Ellie since.*

### 6. Aisha, the Brilliant Undergrad
*The summer student you'd been ignoring just cracked the thing you've been stuck on for a year.*
- **Mentor her properly (half a day/term).** She becomes a high-output, cheap team member.
- **Too busy.** She joins Holloway's lab. *His lab gets stronger; you meet her, thriving, at a conference in 4 terms.*

### 7. The Conference *(uses §3)*
*The big meeting in your field is in Lisbon. The talks start at 8am. The city does not.*
First pick your **slot** (poster / short / full / invited, by Rep), then **who pays** (travel grant / lab / own pocket). Then:
- **Work the talks.** +Rep by tier, a collaboration lead.
- **"Network" on the waterfront.** +Morale, no Rep. We know why people go.
- **Bring Priya.** +Morale and her loyalty rises. The good-boss move.

### 8. "We Regret to Inform You"
*Grant declined. Three reviewers, mutually contradictory, one clearly stopped at the abstract.*
- **Resubmit now (4 weeks, +Workload).** Slightly better odds next round.
- **Pivot the project.** Lose current Knowledge, open a fresh topic.
- **Swear off this funder.** +Morale, that funder closed for the run.

### 9. The p-Hack
*p = 0.06. So close. A nudge and it's a Nature paper.*
- **Report it honestly.** Publish one tier lower. Clean.
- **Massage it.** Top-tier paper, big Rep spike. *Fuse: a retraction event, probability rising each term — if it fires, −huge Rep, can chain into "Misconduct Inquiry."* The spiciest button in the game.

### 10. Imposter Syndrome
*You landed the big one. You're now certain they'll find out you're a fraud.*
- **See someone (£80/session, half a day).** Restore Morale + a buffer against the next hit.
- **Bury it in work.** +Knowledge now, −Morale + Workload later. *Fuse.*
- *If lab Morale is high:* **Talk to Sarah Whitfield.** Free Morale restore. Relationships pay off.

### 11. Dr. Marsh Has Ghosted You
*Stephen Marsh promised the key dataset eight months ago. His out-of-office is from last year.*
- **Keep waiting (lose 2 weeks).** Then 50/50 he delivers.
- **Redo it yourself (4 weeks + £4k).** Guaranteed, exhausting, +Workload.
- **Escalate to his head of department.** Fast, but you've made an enemy. *Fuse risk.*

### 12. Sir Geoffrey's Gift *(rare/legendary)*
*A wealthy alumnus loves your field and will fund you generously. He has one small condition about what you study.*
- **Take it (+£300k research funding).** Locked to safe, low-Rep work for 3 terms.
- **Decline on principle.** +Morale, no money, integrity intact and useless.
- *If Rep ≥ 8:* **Negotiate.** The money *and* your freedom. Reputation has privileges.

### 13. The Haunted Centrifuge *(comic relief / pacing)*
*Sample 7 is always warm. Always. There is a small shrine now.*
- **Buy a new one (£3k).** Solved. No fun.
- **Perform the ritual (free).** +Morale, 50/50 running gag about whether it worked.
- **Ignore it.** Next experiment has a small chance to fail mysteriously.

### 14. The Tenure Whisper
*Margaret Critchley pulls you aside. The committee would like "more service, less self-indulgent research."*
- **Play the game.** Do the service (+2 weeks, +Workload), gain committee standing.
- **Out-publish the politics.** Ignore it, gamble raw Rep carries you at review.
- **Schmooze.** Spend Morale charming the right people; standing without the time cost.

### 15. Burnout *(you did not choose this; it chose you)*
*Triggers at Morale 0 or Workload 100.*
- **Medical leave.** Lose a full term, Morale fully restores.
- **Push through.** Half Time, Morale stays critical. *Fuse: "Leaving Academia," possible run-ender.*
- *If your students are loyal:* **Chloe and Ben keep the lab running while you recover.** The payoff for every time you chose the team over the output.

### 16. Caffeine Crash *(new — the coffee bill)*
*Sixth espresso. Your hands are doing a thing. You read the same sentence four times.*
**Triggers:** after heavy Coffee use.
- **Sleep (lose a day).** Workload drops, Morale steadies.
- **Push on.** One more productive day now, then a bigger Morale hit. The loan compounds.

---

**The quiet argument of the design:** Morale, loyalty, and Workload are the resources that decide long games, and the humane choices keep refilling them. Cynical play wins sprints; kind play wins careers. That's a satire that's also true, and it's a better game than nihilism.
